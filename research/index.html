Depression Detection Using ModernBERT and CNN Ensemble Model in Text Data

Depression and suicidal distress are often reflected in how people write online, which makes social media text a useful source for early risk screening at scale. At the same time, manual assessment and traditional clinical workflows are difficult to apply to large volumes of user-generated content. In this paper, we present a parallel ensemble model that combines ModernBERT and a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) for binary classification of mental-health-related posts. ModernBERT is used to capture long-range contextual meaning in text, while the CNN branch focuses on shorter local patterns such as key phrases and syntactic cues. Instead of relying on separate classifier heads, we extract feature vectors from both branches, concatenate them, apply dropout (p=0.3) to improve robustness, and use a final fully connected layer to produce the class logits. We evaluate the approach on the Kaggle Reddit SuicideWatch dataset (232,074 posts; balanced labels). The model converges quickly and generalizes well: after three epochs it achieves 99.74 % training accuracy (loss 0.0077) and 99.39 % validation accuracy (loss 0.0231). Overall, the proposed ensemble reaches 99.33 % accuracy, demonstrating that combining long-context transformer representations with CNN-based local feature extraction can improve reliability for text-based mental health screening.

Robust Skin Cancer Detection via CNN-Transformer-GRU Fusion and GAN Data Augmentation

Skin cancer remains a significant global health challenge, and early detection is crucial to improving patient outcomes. This study presents a novel deep learning framework that combines Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Transformers, and Gated Recurrent Units (GRUs) for robust skin cancer classification. To address data set imbalance, we employ StyleGAN3-based synthetic data augmentation alongside traditional techniques. The hybrid architecture effectively captures both local and global dependencies in dermoscopic images, while the GRU component models sequential patterns. Evaluated on the HAM10000 dataset, the proposed model achieves an accuracy of 90.61%, outperforming baseline architectures such as VGG16 and ResNet. Our system also demonstrates superior precision (91.11%), recall (95.28%), and AUC (0.97), highlighting its potential as a reliable diagnostic tool for the detection of melanoma. This work advances automated skin cancer diagnosis by addressing critical challenges related to class imbalance and limited generalization in medical imaging.
